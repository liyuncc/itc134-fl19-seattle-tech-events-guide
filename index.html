Why connect to tech communities in Seattle?

  • Foster relationships with new professional contacts
  • Build projects with teams and gain experience
  • Create community with fellow tech industry professionals
  • Refresh existing skills and stay current on trends
  • Learn new skills to stay competitive
  • Find potential job referrals and references
  • Contribute your knowledge and exchange ideas

Focus on your goals to select which events to attend

Questions like these can help narrow down which events are better suited to help you achieve your goals and keep up motivation to continue networking:

  • Are you new to Seattle and just trying to discover people in the industry?
  • If you’re job seeking, are you looking at specific companies or positions?
  • Do you have skillsets you’re trying to grow or share with others?
  • Are you interested in certain programming languages?

They can also help you plan your approach to the event beforehand...

Plan to network

At most networking events, you will have to market yourself in an effective and concise way, so be prepared to pitch yourself!

Be able to speak to your current state in just a few sentences:

  • Where do you work and what do you do?
  • If you’re a student, where and what are you studying?
  • What is your professional history?
  • If what you’ve done diverges from what you’d like to do, why the transition?
  • What do you want?

Practice your responses to standard questions like these so communication is natural, flows easily, and you can divert your energy to unanticipated prompts.

Are you looking to speak to specific individuals?

  • Use resources like LinkedIn to ask about their path into and through the industry
  • Research relevant company websites for details (you may have questions too!)

Draw connections between yourself and the industry and ask questions--conversation lulls happen!

  • Find ways to be relatable and engaging through prepared talking points
  • Think about how current events create ripple events through the industry.
  • Ex: Will the acquisition of X necessitate departmental restructuring?
  • If you’re having difficulty learning concepts, ask questions reminiscent of their learning experience and welcome tips or suggestions.
  • Demonstrate your curiosity through inquiry and good feedback
  • Company blogs and articles can be good ways to check the temperature of certain subjects
